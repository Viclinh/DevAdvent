Profile Tabs Explanation

Under your bio, you can see different tabs such as Project, Hackathons, Achievements, Followers, Following, and Like. Project tab shows the projects that you submitted to hackathons. Hackathons tab shows list of hackathons you registered. Achievements tab shows different achievement of yours. Followers tab shows people who follow you. Following shows people whom you follow. Like tab shows all projects that you liked.

Hackathon Overview

The "Overview" tab of the hackathon gives you the overall information about the challenges, the prizes, the requirements, and the judges.

My Project Tab

The "My Project" tab of the hackathon shows your submitted or drafted project to the hackathon.

Participants Tab

The "Participants" tab of the hackathon shows hackers who joined the hackathon. You can team up with people who are looking for team by sending message to them in this tab.

Resources Tab

The "Resources" tab of the hackathon shows links or materials that can help hackers coomplete their projects for the hackathons.

Rules Tab

Be sure to check the hackathon rules listed on the hackathon page and on the rules tab. You should check your age, your occupation, and your locations. Some hackathons are restricted to certain regions only. They can be open for college students or high school students only. So, make sure you check to see if you are eligible or else you will waste time building your work and then your work is disqualified. Further, hackathons have rules that you should develop your work in certain timeframe so stick to the rules or else you are disqualified.

Project Gallery Tab

The "Project Gallery" tab of the hackathon shows all submitted projects for the hackathons.

Updates Tab

The "Updates" tab of the hackathon shows any updates or announcements from the hackathon managers.

Discussions Tab

In the "Discussions" tab of the hackathon you can post questions and get answers from hackathon managers.

Enter a Submission

Once you’ve registered for a hackathon, you can begin the process of submitting a project. Once on the Overview page of your hackathon, navigate to the sidebar and choose one of two options: Start project or Import from portfolio. Clicking Start project will create a new ‘Untitled’ project–the first step in your hackathon submission. Add or remove teammates on the Manage Team step, and add your project’s basic details such as project name and elevator pitch on Project Overview. Navigate to any step of the submission process by clicking on that stage in the submission nav (Manage team, Project overview, etc.) at the top. The green check mark indicates completed steps. When you’re ready to submit your project, continue to the Submit step and click Submit project. If you still have steps to complete before submitting, an error message will direct you to complete the missing steps.

Import from Portfolio

To submit an existing project from your portfolio, click the Import from portfolio link near the top of the hackathon sidebar. After clicking Import from portfolio, select one of your pre-existing projects that you’d like to submit. Upon selecting the project, you’ll be taken to the Project details step and your project will now be a Draft submission in the hackathon.
